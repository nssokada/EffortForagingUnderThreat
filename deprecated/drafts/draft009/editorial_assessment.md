# Editorial Assessment: Draft 009

This paper presents a coherent and novel contribution linking computational foraging models to clinical anxiety through a metacognitive decomposition, and the restructured Results section now flows naturally from static model to within-trial dynamics to affect to clinical pathways. The two-pathway clinical dissociation (discrepancy to anxiety, encounter reactivity to apathy) is the strongest conceptual advance over prior drafts and is now clearly articulated as a standalone finding. At approximately 7,400 words, the manuscript sits within the acceptable range for a Nature Communications Article, though further trimming of the Methods section (particularly model fitting details that could move to Supplement) would strengthen the submission. The main remaining risks are (a) statistical discrepancies between the hypothesis documents and the manuscript text (Table 2 ROPE values and some correlation coefficients need final verification against analysis notebooks), and (b) the paper's reliance on a single discovery sample---the confirmatory replication is essential and should be completed before submission. Overall, the manuscript is near submission-ready, pending confirmation of statistics against source notebooks and completion of the preregistered replication.

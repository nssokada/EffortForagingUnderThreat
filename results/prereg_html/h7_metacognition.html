H7 — Individuals who mobilize vigor under danger show more accurate subjective threat appraisal

Figure. (A) Participants with higher danger mobilization ($\delta$) show steeper negative anxiety–$S$ slopes ($r = -0.311$), indicating their anxiety ratings more accurately track survival probability. (B) The same pattern for confidence: higher $\delta$ predicts steeper positive confidence–$S$ slopes ($r = +0.325$). $N = 293$.

Statement: Participants whose motor effort is more danger-responsive (higher $\delta$) will show tighter affective tracking of survival probability $S$ — steeper anxiety–$S$ and confidence–$S$ slopes — constituting more accurate metacognitive appraisal of threat.
H7a.1 — $r(\delta,\, \text{anxiety slope on } S) < 0$, $p < .05$ one-tailed.
H7a.2 — $r(\delta,\, \text{confidence slope on } S) > 0$, $p < .05$ one-tailed.
H7a.3 (secondary) — $r(\delta,\, \text{mean anxiety}) < 0$: high-$\delta$ individuals report lower average anxiety despite stronger coupling.

H2 — Choice shift and vigor shift are inversely correlated

Exploratory sample, $N = 293$

Hypothesis: Choice shift and vigor shift under threat will be inversely correlated across individuals — participants who shift choices most toward safety will show the largest increase in excess effort.
H2a — Pearson $r(\Delta\text{choice}, \Delta\text{vigor}) < 0$, $p < .01$ one-tailed.
H2b — Split-half robustness: $r(\Delta\text{choice}_{\text{odd}}, \Delta\text{vigor}_{\text{even}}) < 0$, $p < .05$. Same for reversed split.

Figure. Across-subject coupling between threat-driven choice shift ($\Delta$choice) and vigor shift ($\Delta$vigor). Participants who reduce high-effort choices more under threat also increase their pressing rate more. $N = 293$.

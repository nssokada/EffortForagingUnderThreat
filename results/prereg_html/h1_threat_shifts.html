H1 — Threat reduces high-effort choice, amplifies avoidance, and shifts affect

Exploratory sample, $N = 293$ · 13,185 choice trials

Hypothesis: Threat will reduce high-effort choice, amplify distance-driven avoidance, and increase excess motor effort — while shifting anxiety upward and confidence downward.
H1a — Logistic LMM: choice ~ threat_z + dist_z + threat_z:dist_z + (1|subj). All $\beta < 0$, $p < .01$, plus monotonicity of $P(\text{high})$ across adjacent threat levels within each distance.
H1b — Linear LMM: excess_effort ~ threat_z × dist_z + effort_chosen_z + (1|subj). $\beta(\text{threat}) > 0$; $\beta(\text{threat} \times \text{dist}) < 0$.
H1c — LMMs: anxiety ~ threat_z + dist_z + (1+threat_z|subj): $\beta > 0$. Confidence: $\beta < 0$. Both $p < .001$.

Figure. Threat shifts choice, effort, and affect. (A) P(choose high-effort) decreases with threat and distance. (B) Excess motor effort increases with threat. (C–D) Anxiety increases and confidence decreases with threat probability. $N = 293$.

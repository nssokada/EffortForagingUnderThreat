H5 — Model-derived danger drives excess motor vigor

Exploratory sample, $N = 293$ · MCMC (NumPyro NUTS, 4 chains × 1000) · $\lambda = 0.698$

Statement: Lower survival probability $S$ causes participants to press harder than the task requires, expressed as a positive population-mean danger mobilization parameter $\delta$ in a hierarchical Bayesian model of excess effort, after controlling for the physical effort demand of the chosen option.
H5a.1 — Population mean $\mu_\delta > 0$; the posterior 95% credible interval must exclude zero.
H5a.2 — The proportion of subjects with posterior mean $\delta_i > 0$ must exceed 80%.
Secondary — $\sigma_\delta > 0.05$ (individual differences recoverable) and $\gamma < 0$ (effort demand constraint confirmed).
Vigor model
$$\text{excess}_{ij} = \alpha_i + \delta_i \cdot (1 - S_{ij}) + \gamma \cdot E_{\text{chosen},ij} + \varepsilon_{ij}$$
$S_{ij} = (1 - T) + T / (1 + \lambda D_{\text{chosen}})$  |  $\alpha_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_\alpha, \sigma_\alpha)$  |  $\delta_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_\delta, \sigma_\delta)$

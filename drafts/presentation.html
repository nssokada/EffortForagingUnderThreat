Humans Reallocate Effort Across
Decision and Action When Foraging Under Threat

Okada, Garg, Wise, Mobbs  |  Exploratory N=293  |  Confirmatory N=350 (collected)

As threat increases, people choose safer targets while pressing harder on them — coordinated reallocation governed by a shared survival computation S.

H1 — Survival-weighted value model governs choice

5 models, each testing one structural question. M5 wins.

SV = R·S − k·E − β·(1−S)
S = (1−T) + T/(1+λD)
  • Additive > multiplicative: ΔELBO = +158
  • Hyperbolic > exponential: ΔELBO = +174
  • Survival > effort-only: ΔELBO = +2,038
  • Accuracy 76.1%, AUC 0.863
  • k and β independent (r = −0.02)
  • λ = 14.0 (fixed; Supp Note 1)
  • MCMC validated: Rhat = 1.00, 0 divergences

H6 — Vigor mobilization predicts metacognitive accuracy

People who press harder under danger have more accurately calibrated feelings.

  • δ × anxiety-S slope: r = −0.31
  • δ × confidence-S slope: r = +0.33
  • β shows same pattern (|r| > 0.28)
  • k predicts calibration (r = +0.20)
  • α does NOT predict calibration

δ × mean anxiety: r = −0.19

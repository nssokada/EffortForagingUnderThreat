H1 — Survival-weighted additive-effort model wins

SV = R · S − k · E − β · (1 − S)
S  = (1 − T) + T / (1 + λ · D)

5-model comparison

ModelStructureQuestion
M1SV = R·exp(−kE)Does threat matter?
M2SV = R·exp(−kE) − β·T·DMechanistic S or features?
M3Mult × exponential SWhich kernel?
M4Mult × hyperbolic SWhich effort structure?
M5Additive × hyperbolic SWinner

Primary tests

  • M5 > M4 (additive > mult): ΔELBO = +158
  • M4 > M3 (hyp > exp): ΔELBO = +174
  • M5 > M1 (survival > effort-only): ΔELBO = +2,038

Parameters (MCMC-validated)

  • k: median = 4.25, M = 6.22
  • β: median = 53.5, M = 81.8
  • k–β: r = −0.02 (independent)
  • λ = 14.0 (fixed), τ = 0.76

Supporting evidence

PPCs: all 9 conditions within HDI. Calibration near diagonal. Vigor PPC tracks threat.

H2 — S predicts trial-level anxiety and confidence

Primary tests

  • Anxiety ~ S_probe: β = −0.281, z = −24.1
  • Confidence ~ S_probe: β = +0.280, z = +23.7

Secondary (parameter moderation)

  • k → mean anxiety: β = +0.127, p = .032
  • k → mean confidence: β = −0.154, p = .009
Cross-domain: Choice threat shift × anxiety shift r = −0.386; × confidence shift r = +0.372

Supporting evidence (panels A–B)

A: Anxiety decreases with S. B: Confidence increases with S. 3 threat levels.

MCMC validation

Supporting evidence

H4 — Choice and vigor shift coherently under threat

H4.1 — Population independence

  • P(choose high) × α: r = +0.008, p = .894 confirmed

H4.2 — k dominates choice

  • Choice ~ k + β + α: adj.R² = 0.823, k dominant

H4.3 — Threat reverses coupling

  • r(choice, vigor | T=0.1) = +0.196
  • r(choice, vigor | T=0.9) = −0.219
  • Fisher z = 5.07, p < .0001 confirmed

H4.4 — Vigor predicts escape

  • Vigor β = +0.091, p = 10⁻⁷⁷; Choice β = −0.177

Supporting evidence

A: Dual-axis shift. B: Choice-vigor coupling (r = −0.78). C: β vs δ. D: Quadrants. E: Regression.

H6 — δ predicts metacognitive accuracy

Primary tests

  • δ × anxiety-S slope: r = −0.311, p < .001
  • δ × confidence-S slope: r = +0.325, p < .001

Secondary

  • β shows same pattern (|r| > 0.28)
  • k predicts calibration (r = +0.20)
  • α does NOT predict calibration
"Adaptive, not anxious": δ × mean anxiety r = −0.194. High-δ people have lower tonic anxiety but steeper phasic response. Calibrated, not chronically anxious.

Supporting evidence (panels C–F)

C: δ → anxiety calibration. D: δ → confidence calibration. E: Parameter bars. F: Adaptive, not anxious.

Supplementary: Mental health (null, ROPE-confirmed)

The one bridge: α → Apathy

  • α → F3 (Apathy): R² = 0.12, p < .001
  • Driven by AMI Social (r = +0.18)

Everything else: null

  • δ, β, coupling → all 3 factors: null
  • Calibration → all factors: null
  • Depressed (PHQ≥10) show same coupling as controls
  • ROPE: P(|effect| < 0.1) > 0.70 for most associations
Interpretation: The reallocation system predicts foraging performance, not psychopathology. It's a performance phenotype.

Supporting evidence

A: Factor loadings. B: Bayesian forest. C: Per-scale heatmap. D: α → Apathy. E: ROPE testing.
